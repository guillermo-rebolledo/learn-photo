# Night Sky visual review

Reviewed 2026-07-19 against the 960 px local derivative in light and dark interface themes.

- 30 seconds, f/2.8, ISO 3200: usable reference brightness; stars remain point-like enough to communicate the qualified “relatively sharp” intention; noise is visible without obscuring the sky.
- 1 minute: the source-point-derived arc layer introduces a short trace, distinct from the 30-second state.
- 2 minutes: visible but short trails support a Close evaluation for deliberate trails.
- 5 minutes, f/4, ISO 400: extended arcs make deliberate motion clear while the lower ISO keeps noise restrained.
- 10 minutes, f/4, ISO 200: the longest arcs remain legible over the Source Photograph and provide a second valid trail solution.
- Dark/bright extremes: the calibrated brightness range retains the silhouetted foreground and readable sky structure without presenting the result as a camera-certified prediction.
- Graceful degradation: disabling visual effects removes trail/noise layers and filter adjustment; synchronized duration, exposure, motion, noise text, evaluation, and controls remain usable.
- Reduced motion: the trail layer changes between static deterministic states with transitions disabled; no long-running capture or countdown animation occurs.

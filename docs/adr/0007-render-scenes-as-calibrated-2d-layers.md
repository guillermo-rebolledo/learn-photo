# Render scenes as calibrated 2D layers

The Conceptual Simulator will deterministically derive each Rendered Result from a real Source Photograph, scene-specific subject, depth, motion, and highlight masks, and stops-based exposure calculations. It may apply calibrated brightness, defocus, directional motion, noise or grain, and derive the Histogram from the result; every scene must be visually reviewed across representative settings, and generic filters alone will not be treated as a credible simulation. This avoids a combinatorial library of prerendered images and the weight of a 3D engine while preserving believable cause-and-effect rather than camera-certified prediction. Unsupported visual effects must degrade without blocking the learner: controls, evaluation, Histogram, and textual outcomes remain usable, the viewfinder uses a representative fallback when possible, and the app explicitly identifies any effect it cannot show rather than presenting a misleading result.

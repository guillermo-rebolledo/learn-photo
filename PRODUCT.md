# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Beginners who want to understand camera fundamentals, including people considering their first film or digital camera and camera owners who want to move beyond automatic operation. They may use the responsive web app while learning at home or alongside a physical camera, and need the controls to feel approachable rather than overwhelming.

## Product Purpose

Teach Exposure Fundamentals through a recommended but unlocked Learning Path and a realistic-looking Conceptual Simulator. The free, public MVP helps learners predict how aperture, shutter speed, and ISO affect an image, choose settings for a Photographic Intention, understand tradeoffs, and transfer that mental model to both digital-first and film-constrained situations.

## Brand Personality

Clear, encouraging, and tactile. The product should replace intimidation with calm curiosity and make every interaction feel directly connected to a photographic consequence.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble a dense camera manual, a sterile corporate course platform, or a childish gamified learning app. Avoid unexplained technical dashboards, punitive grading, decorative complexity, camera-brand mimicry, and interfaces that make experimentation feel risky.

## Design Principles

- Show cause and effect: connect every control adjustment to a visible outcome and plain-language explanation.
- Guide without gating: recommend a coherent path while keeping lessons and the Sandbox freely accessible.
- Explain tradeoffs, not answers: assess intentions across independent criteria and accept multiple valid solutions.
- Reveal complexity progressively: begin with full stops and isolated concepts, then introduce camera-realistic scales, modes, and exceptions.
- Earn realism: use credible photographic scenes while stating the Conceptual Simulator's assumptions and limits.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Every feature must work with touch, mouse, and keyboard; nothing may depend on hover or require a physical keyboard. Provide visible focus, sufficient contrast, comfortably sized targets, reduced-motion behavior, screen-reader labels, non-color status cues, text and zoom resilience, and textual equivalents for visual effects such as blur, noise, histogram shape, and exposure changes. Use plain English and define photography terminology as it appears.

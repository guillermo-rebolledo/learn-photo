# Photography Learning

This context describes the language used to teach camera fundamentals without assuming prior photography experience.

## Language

**Learner**:
A beginner who wants to understand camera fundamentals, including someone considering their first camera and someone who wants to move beyond automatic operation.
_Avoid_: Photographer, student, user

**Camera**:
A film or digital photographic camera whose settings can be deliberately controlled by the learner.
_Avoid_: Gear, device

**Exposure Fundamentals**:
The initial learning scope: light and exposure, stops, aperture, shutter speed, ISO, metering, basic histogram reading, exposure modes, equivalent exposures, and choosing settings for an intended result.
_Avoid_: Photography basics, camera basics, beginner photography

**Exposure Control**:
One of aperture, shutter speed, or ISO when deliberately adjusted to influence exposure and the resulting image.
_Avoid_: Exposure-triangle setting, manual setting

**Learning Loop**:
The repeating sequence in which a learner receives a concise explanation, experiments with a guided simulation, attempts a challenge, and receives immediate feedback.
_Avoid_: Lesson flow, tutorial cycle

**Challenge**:
An assessment that asks the learner to choose settings for a stated photographic intention in a given scene.
_Avoid_: Quiz, test, exercise

**Sandbox**:
An unrestricted simulation where the learner can vary exposure controls without a prescribed challenge, available separately from the guided Learning Loop.
_Avoid_: Playground, free mode

**Conceptual Simulator**:
An educational approximation that preserves the important relationships between exposure controls and visible outcomes without claiming to reproduce a particular camera, lens, or scene exactly.
_Avoid_: Camera emulator, virtual camera

**Curated Scene**:
A prepared photographic situation used by the Conceptual Simulator to demonstrate exposure, depth of field, motion blur, and noise or grain under controlled conditions.
_Avoid_: Sample image, preset

**Digital-First Model**:
The primary teaching model, in which ISO can be selected for each photograph; film differences are taught explicitly without requiring a separate film-camera simulation.
_Avoid_: Universal camera model, camera-agnostic model

**Film Constraint**:
A challenge condition in which ISO is fixed by the selected film speed, requiring the learner to work with aperture and shutter speed as they would across a roll of film.
_Avoid_: Film mode, analog mode

**Photographic Intention**:
The desired visible result stated by a Challenge, such as freezing motion, preserving depth of field, or limiting image noise.
_Avoid_: Objective, target look, correct answer

**Success Criterion**:
One independently evaluated quality of a Challenge result: usable exposure, intended motion rendering, intended depth of field, or ISO compatible with the image-quality goal.
_Avoid_: Answer, requirement, score component

**Tradeoff Feedback**:
An explanation of which Success Criteria a chosen exposure satisfies, nearly satisfies, or misses, and how changing one Exposure Control would affect the others.
_Avoid_: Correction, answer explanation, grading

**Criterion Status**:
The qualitative assessment of one Success Criterion: Achieved when satisfied, Close when usable with a noticeable compromise, or Missed when the result conflicts with the Photographic Intention or is unusable.
_Avoid_: Points, percentage, grade

**Learning Path**:
The recommended order of lessons and Challenges, with completion and next-step guidance but no restrictions on accessing other content or the Sandbox.
_Avoid_: Course, level system, locked progression

**Progress**:
The learner's record of completed lessons and Challenges and their current recommended next step on the Learning Path.
_Avoid_: Account, profile, grade

**Lesson**:
A short unit in the Learning Path that introduces one Exposure Fundamentals concept through an explanation, a guided experiment, and one or two Challenges.
_Avoid_: Chapter, class, module

**Captured Light**:
The light recorded by the sensor or film, governed by scene illumination, aperture, and shutter speed.
_Avoid_: Brightness, ISO exposure

**Rendered Brightness**:
How light or dark the resulting image appears after the captured signal or exposed film is interpreted, including the influence of ISO and processing.
_Avoid_: Captured light, exposure

**Exposure Triangle**:
A familiar shorthand for aperture, shutter speed, and ISO that the curriculum acknowledges but does not use as its primary model because the three controls are not physically equivalent.
_Avoid_: Using “exposure triangle” as the canonical teaching model

**Stop**:
A relative unit for a doubling or halving: one stop of aperture or shutter changes Captured Light by a factor of two, while one stop of ISO changes the rendered response by a factor of two.
_Avoid_: Step, notch, level

**Beginner Scale**:
The full-stop control scale used in early guided experiments to make doubling, halving, and equivalent settings apparent.
_Avoid_: Easy mode, simplified settings

**Camera Scale**:
The third-stop control scale introduced later and available in the Sandbox to reflect the finer increments commonly offered by real cameras.
_Avoid_: Advanced mode, professional settings

**Available Light**:
The fixed illumination already present in a Curated Scene, to which the learner responds by choosing camera settings.
_Avoid_: Lighting setup, studio lighting, adjustable light

**Meter Reference**:
The exposure meter's estimate for rendering the measured scene near a neutral brightness; useful evidence for choosing settings, but not a definition of a correct image.
_Avoid_: Correct exposure, target exposure, answer

**Exposure Mode**:
A camera's division of responsibility for selecting aperture and shutter speed, taught as Auto, Program, Aperture Priority, Shutter Priority, or Manual with common manufacturer aliases.
_Avoid_: Scene mode, shooting preset

**Exposure Compensation**:
An intentional instruction to an automatic or priority Exposure Mode to render the image brighter or darker than the Meter Reference.
_Avoid_: Meter correction, brightness slider

**Auto ISO**:
An optional digital-camera behavior that lets the camera select ISO within configured limits; introduced after manual ISO so its tradeoffs remain visible.
_Avoid_: Automatic exposure

**Histogram**:
A luminance graph showing how image tones are distributed from darker values on the left to brighter values on the right; its shape describes the image but does not define correctness or analyze separate color channels.
_Avoid_: Exposure graph, brightness score

**Clipping**:
Tones recorded at an image's darkest or brightest limit, potentially losing distinguishable detail in those areas.
_Avoid_: Bad exposure, histogram failure

**Scene Assumptions**:
The fixed format, focal length, focus distance, camera stability, and subject behavior that shape a Curated Scene but are not controlled by the learner.
_Avoid_: Camera configuration, advanced settings

**Capstone**:
The minimally guided, three-part conclusion of the Learning Path in which the learner satisfies essential motion, depth-of-field, and low-light Success Criteria, including one Film Constraint.
_Avoid_: Final exam, certification, graded test

**Attempt**:
A set of exposure choices deliberately submitted with “Take photo” in a Challenge, after which Criterion Statuses and Tradeoff Feedback are revealed and the result can be compared with a later retry.
_Avoid_: Answer, guess, submission

**Reference**:
A learner-facing collection of concise definitions and quick tables for stops, exposure modes, and film differences, available outside the Learning Path.
_Avoid_: Lesson, manual, documentation

**Source Photograph**:
A real, licensed photograph from which a Curated Scene and its controlled visual variations are derived, with its creator and usage rights recorded.
_Avoid_: AI-generated image, placeholder image, unverified royalty-free image

**Rendered Result**:
The deterministic simulated photograph produced from a Curated Scene, its Scene Assumptions, and the current exposure choices.
_Avoid_: Real capture, camera emulation, answer

**Curriculum Source**:
An authoritative reference supporting a technical claim in a Lesson or Challenge, exposed to learners through lesson notes and the Reference.
_Avoid_: Affiliate article, unsourced blog, forum answer

**Bulb Exposure**:
A Night Sky bonus setting that holds the simulated shutter open for a selected long duration beyond the standard 30-second limit, assuming stable camera support and a remote or delayed release.
_Avoid_: Real-time timer, standard shutter-speed setting

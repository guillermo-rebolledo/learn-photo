## Aperture shapes light and depth

Aperture is the adjustable opening in a lens. A smaller f-number means a wider opening: it records more **Captured Light** and usually gives a shallower depth of field. A larger f-number means a narrower opening: it records less Captured Light and usually keeps more distances acceptably defined.

This is a relative relationship, not a universal recipe. The same f-number does not create the same-looking depth of field for every Camera. Format, focal length, and camera-to-subject distance all matter, as do focus distance and the distances within the scene.

In this Conceptual Simulator, Available Light and geometry remain fixed. Change aperture, then balance Captured Light with shutter speed or ISO when you attempt the Challenge.

The Source Photograph was recorded at a wide aperture, so the narrow-aperture rendering preserves the background detail present in that photograph; it cannot reconstruct detail the Camera never recorded. Treat the preview as a calibrated comparison of **relative** softening, not a lens prediction.

### On film

When film speed is fixed for the roll, choosing aperture for depth may require a compensating shutter change. A Film Constraint leaves both controls available but does not let ISO rescue each photograph independently.

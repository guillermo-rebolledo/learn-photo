## Read the image, not a rule

The **Meter Reference** is the Camera’s estimate for settings that render what it measured near a neutral brightness. The mark at zero is useful evidence, but it does not know the **Photographic Intention**. A naturally bright snowfield may need to sit above zero; a dark stage may need to sit below it.

A luminance **Histogram** describes the **Rendered Result**. Darker tones collect on the left, brighter tones on the right, and the height shows how many image pixels share each brightness. This Lesson keeps that evidence luminance-only: it does not split the image into red, green, and blue channels.

The Histogram’s shape is not a score. Bright Snow can reasonably lean right, while a Dark Stage can reasonably lean left. What matters is whether that distribution supports the intended image.

**Clipping** appears when tones reach the darkest or brightest image limit. Those pixels may lose distinguishable detail. Clipping is a reason to inspect the image—not an automatic failure. A small clipped stage light can coexist with a successful dark-stage photograph, while broad clipped snow may undermine the intention.

## One Stop is one doubling or halving

A **Stop** describes a ratio: one Stop more doubles a quantity, and one Stop less halves it. Moving ISO 400 to ISO 800 doubles its rendered response. Moving from 1/125s to about 1/60s doubles how long light is captured.

This shared Stop vocabulary does not make the controls physically identical. **Aperture and shutter speed change Captured Light; digital ISO changes Rendered Brightness** after capture. Aperture also changes depth of field, shutter speed changes motion rendering, and higher ISO can make noise more visible.

Equivalent exposures combine opposite Stop changes to preserve overall brightness. They are alternatives, not duplicates: each combination carries a different photographic tradeoff.

### On film

Stops still describe doublings and halvings, but film speed belongs to the loaded roll. Once that roll is chosen, equivalent exposures must usually be found with aperture and shutter speed rather than changing ISO for each photograph.

## Shutter speed shapes time

Shutter speed controls how long the Camera records light. A slower shutter records more **Captured Light** and more of a moving subject’s travel; a faster shutter records less time and less travel.

Neither result is automatically better. Freezing motion can clarify a fleeting instant, while deliberate motion can make movement visible. The useful choice follows the **Photographic Intention**.

The motion bands below belong to this Curated Scene. A real result also depends on the cyclist’s speed and direction, focal length, viewing size, Camera movement, and distance—not on one universal shutter threshold.

### On film

With one film speed loaded across the roll, a faster shutter for freezing motion must usually be balanced by a wider aperture or more Available Light. The ISO control is not a per-photograph escape hatch.

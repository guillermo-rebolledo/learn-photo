## Decide who makes each choice

An **Exposure Mode** divides responsibility between the Learner and the Camera. In **Auto**, the Camera chooses aperture, shutter speed, and ISO. **Program (`P`)** chooses aperture and shutter speed while leaving ISO with you. **Aperture Priority (`A` or `Av`)** preserves your aperture and chooses shutter speed. **Shutter Priority (`S` or `Tv`)** preserves your shutter speed and chooses aperture. **Manual (`M`)** preserves both.

These modes do not change what aperture, shutter speed, or ISO do. They change who selects them. A mode is useful when it protects the Exposure Control that matters most to your **Photographic Intention**.

**Exposure Compensation** tells an automatic or priority mode to place its result above or below the **Meter Reference**. It is not a brightness slider and does not guarantee the intended image: the Camera can reach a limit, and you still judge the Rendered Result.

Digital cameras may offer **Auto ISO**. When enabled, the Camera can change ISO within configured limits. This Lesson keeps ISO manual first so its image-quality tradeoff stays visible, then introduces Auto ISO as an optional behavior.
